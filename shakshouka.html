Meatballs with Eggs : Shaksouka

Shakshuka is a very common Middle Eastern dish and originally from a country that has had a persistent passion for eggs and tomatoes. The Moroccan version of the shakshuka includes kefta meatballscooked in the tomato-based sauce along with the eggs, and in Morocco they don't call it shakshuka but simply "kefta and egg tagine". The result is a hearty and satisfying dish that combines the rich flavors of the tomato sauce with the savory meatballs and perfectly poached eggs. It is often served with moroccan crusty bread or Batbout the delicious sauce.

Prep time

15 min

Cook time

30min

Tags : Dinner

instructions

step 1

• Shape the kefta into small meatballs (2,5 cm diameter), cover and keep in the fridge.

step 2

• In a large frying pan (or a Tajine) stir in the rest of the ingredients, except the eggs, over medium heat. Bring to the boil and cover with a lid under low heat. Let simmer until it becomes a thick and fragrant tomato sauce, about 25min.

step 3

• Transfer the meatballs in the sauce and cover until they are almost cooked, about 7 to 9 min.

step 4

• Make some room for the eggs and gently break them into the sauce. Cover with a lid until the eggs are poached; egg white set and yolk still runny (4 to 5 minutes).

step 5

• Garnish with fresh chopped coriander and serve immediately with bread.

ingredients

450g kefta : ground beef

2 tablespoons olive oil

2 garlic cloves, peeled and finely chopped

1 can peeled tomatoes (400g/14oz.) or 5 ripe tomatoes, chopped

1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley

1 teaspoon ground cumin

1 teaspoon paprika

Salt and pepper to taste

4 eggs

1 teaspoon chopped fresh coriander for garnish

tools

Large frying pan

Measuring Spoons
