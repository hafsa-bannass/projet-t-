Baghrir

Baghrir is a type of pancake that originates from Morocco. It is made from a batter that typically includes semolina flour, yeast, and water, which is then cooked on a griddle or pan. The surface of the pancake features many small holes, giving it a unique texture that is slightly spongy and porous. Baghrir is often served with butter, honey, or jam as a sweet breakfast or snack, and it is usually eaten with Moroccan mint tea.

Prep time

10 min

Rest

30 min

Cook time

15 min

Tags : Tea Time

instructions

step 1

• In a blender insert the semolina, flour, sugar, yeast and salt. Add the warm water and blend until there are no lumps and the batter is smooth. If you don't have a blender place all the ingredients in a large bowl and use an electric whisk instead of a blender.

step 2

• Add the baking powder and blend again for a few seconds. Leave the batter for 30 minutes to allow the yeast to proof and blend again for a few seconds.

step 3

• Grease a non-stick pan over medium high-heat. Wait for the pan to be very hot to start baking the pancakes, otherwise you won't get many holes on your pancakes.

step 4

• Pour about 1/2 cup of batter in the warm pan and leave the pancake until it dries out. As soon as you place the batter on the pan, the batter should start bubbling and drying out. Once the baghrir is no longer wet (it takes about a minute), it will mean that it is cooked. The baghrir are cooked only on one side. Never flip them while baking them, as you will loose the holes. Repeat until you've used all the batter.

step 5

• Don't pile up your Moroccan pancakes while they are still hot, as they will stick to each other. If you want to pile them when they are hot, separate them with a tissue.

ingredients

250 gr of fine semolina

4 tablespoons plain flour (40 gr)

2 tablespoons caster sugar

1 heaped teaspoon dry yeast

½ teaspoon salt

500 ml warm water

2 teaspoons baking powder

tools

Blender

Non-stick pan
