Moroccan cookie : Bahla

Bahla is a traditional Moroccan cookie that is popular during special occasions and holidays, such as Eid al-Fitr and weddings. Bahla cookies are known for their delicate texture and slightly sweet flavor, with a subtle nutty taste from the almonds. They are often served with tea or coffee and can be stored in an airtight container for several days.

Prep time

15 min

Cook time

15-20 min

Tags : Tea Time

instructions

step 1: Preping the Seasame and Almonds

• Toast the unhulled sesame seeds. Preheat your oven to 400° F (200° C). Spread the sesame seeds in a single layer on a baking sheet and place in the preheated oven for about 10 minutes. Let cool before using.

• Toast (or fry) the blanched almonds, then grind. To toast, spread the blanched almonds on a baking sheet and place in a 350° F (180° C) oven for about 15 to 20 minutes, until pale golden. (To fry, heat a shallow layer of vegetable oil in a small pan over medium heat; add the almonds and stir constantly until golden, then transfer to a paper towel lined plate.) When the almonds are cool, coarsely grind them.

step 2

• In a large bowl, combine the sugar, butter and oil. Mix well.Stir in the ground almonds and toasted sesame seeds.

step 3

• Combine the flour, baking powder, and salt in a separate medium bowl. Add it to the eggs and sugar mixture and stir lightly to combine.

step 4

• Add half of the flour and all of the baking powder then mix until well-combined. Use your hands to work in as much of the remaining flour as needed to get a very crumbly, dry mixture.

step 5

• Use a stand mixer and paddle attachment to mix the dough on the lowest speed for 10 minutes, or knead by hand for 20 minutes, to make a dough that clumps together but is not so moist as to form one uniform mass. If the dough seems to be too moist, add a little flour. Conversely, if the dough remains too dry and crumbly to easily pack into a ball, then add a tablespoon or two of oil. In either case, allow ample mixing time to blend any additions fully throughout the dough

step 6

• Preheat your oven to 338° F (170° C). Line a regular baking sheet with parchment paper.

step 7

• Take a small portion of dough and squeeze it in your hand to compress it and make it easy to mold. Shape it into a 1 1/2" (3.5 cm) ball, then flatten the ball to a smooth disc shape. Correct any cracked edges.

step 8

• Place the disc on your prepared pan. Repeat with the remaining dough. Plan to bake the cookies in batches, one pan at a time. Place the baking rack to its lowest position and turn on the broiler.

step 9

• Bake the cookies for 5 or 6 minutes, then turn off the broiler and move the cookies to the upper third of the oven. Continue baking for another 15 minutes, or until the cookies are lightly colored with crackled tops.

step 10

• Remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool a few minutes on the pan. Transfer them to a rack to cool completely before storing in an airtight container with wax paper or plastic between layers.

ingredients

2/3 cup granulated sugar

1/2 cup unsalted butter, - softened

1/2 cup vegetable oil

1/4 cup unhulled sesame seeds - toasted

1/4 cup whole blanched almonds, - toasted then ground

1 teaspoon vanilla - (or 2 teaspoons vanilla sugar)

3 teaspoons baking powder

½ teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon vanilla
