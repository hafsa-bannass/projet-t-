Roasted Eggplants: Zaalook

Zaalouk is a Moroccan cooked salad of eggplants and tomatoes that's usually eaten as a dip with Moroccan bread. Key to zaalouk's great flavor is the traditional Moroccan seasoning of cumin and paprika with garlic, cilantro, parsley, and olive oil. A little lemon juice can be added to lift the flavor at end of cooking.

Prep time

15 min

Cook time

20-30min

Tags : Vegetarian Dishes

instructions

step 1

• Preheat your oven to 200°C/400°F.

• Cut the eggplants into small cubes and place them on a baking sheet. Drizzle with 1 tablespoon of olive oil and sprinkle with salt and black pepper. Toss to coat the eggplant pieces evenly.

step 2

• Roast the eggplant in the oven for 15 minutes or until they are soft and slightly browned.

step 3

• In a large pan, heat the remaining olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute.

step 4

• Add the chopped tomatoes and tomato paste to the pan. Stir well and cook for 5-10 minutes or until the tomatoes are soft and well combined.

step 5

• Add the roasted eggplant cubes to the tomato mixture, along with the cumin, paprika, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt. Stir well to combine and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.

step 6

• Using a fork, mash the eggplant and tomato mixture until it reaches a coarse texture.

step 7

• Add chopped fresh parsley to the pan and stir well.

Taste and adjust the seasoning, if needed.

step 8

• Enjoy Zaalouk hot or cold as a side dish or as a dip with Moroccan bread.

ingredients

2 medium-sized eggplants

4 ripe tomatoes, peeled and chopped

2 garlic cloves, minced

1 tablespoon tomato paste

1 teaspoon ground cumin

1 teaspoon paprika

Salt and black pepper, to taste

2 tablespoons olive oil

1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Backing oven sheet
